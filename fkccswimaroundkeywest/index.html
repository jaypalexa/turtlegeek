The Next FKCC Swim Around Key West will be
Saturday, June 10, 2017

The Only Officially Sanctioned, Insured and US Coast Guard Permitted Swim Race around Key West

The Florida keys Community College Swim Around Key West is a 12.5 mile journey around the pristine island of Key West.

The race will begin at Higgs Beach (1000 Atlantic Blvd, Key West, FL 33040) and head towards the southernmost point to the Key West Harbor. The course continues through the harbor and under Fleming Key Bridge, through the City of Key West mooring field, around Sigsbee, and on to Cow Key Bridge. Past Cow Key Bridge the swimmers will make their way around towards the Airport and past Smathers Beach and the White Street Pier to the finish line at Higgs Beach.

The FKCC Swim Around Key West is swum in two different oceans: The Gulf of Mexico and Atlantic Ocean. You can see lots of aquatic life and maybe a mermaid or two.

This is a warm water swim with temperatures from 78 degrees to 84 degrees Fahrenheit.

You can compete in either a solo category or team categories of 2 people up to 6 person relays. Every swimmer/team must have their own escort craft in the form of kayaks or motor boats.

All safety crew boats will be flying Pirate Flags and Lifeguards are positioned throughout the course.
